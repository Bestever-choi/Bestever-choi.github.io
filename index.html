Learning Dynamic Manipulation Skills from Haptic-Play
Kyoungyeon Choi
Joint work with Taeyoon Lee, Donghyun Sung, Choongin Lee, Changwoo Park and Keunjun Choi during NAVER LABS internship.
Submitted to IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters(RA-L) and IROS 2022.
Presented at 2nd NAVER LABS Europe International Workshop on AI for Robotics
slide

Proposed a practical data-driven skill learning approach to solve highly dynamic manipulation tasks entirely from offline teleoperated play data. By learning the state conditional latent skill distribution and skill conditional state transition dynamics, it allows model-based planning in the learned skill-space to accomplish any given downstream tasks at test time.

Learning 6-dof grasping from partial point cloud embedding
Kyoungyeon Choi
Advisor: Prof. Frank Chongwoo Park

Trained a grasping policy that selects a successful grasp pose from a VAE grasp generator by learning point cloud embedding and offset actions. The policy learns to output a latent action that encodes the desired SE(3) of a gripper utilizing an antipodal cost and soft actor-critic algorithm.

Recovery controller for ANYmal on various terrains
Kyoungyeon Choi
Advisor: Prof. Marco Hutter
project page

Developed a recovery controller for quadruped robot ”ANYmal”, enabling the robot to recover and stand up on sloped terrains. Applying terrain randomization, normal-estimation, and cost function design, ANYmal learned to recover from arbitrary configurations on challenging terrains only with proprioceptive measurements.

Two-mode Fiber with a Reduced Mode Overlap for Uncoupled Mode-division Multiplexing in C+L Band
